FEATURE

The new 124.4cc single-cylinder air-cooled counter-balanced mill on the Pulsar 125 produces 11.8PS at 8500rpm and 10.8Nm at 6500rpm after adhering to the BS6 emission norms. You still find a five-speed gearbox on the Bajaj Pulsar 125. The double-cradle frame of the Bajaj Pulsar 125 is suspended by means of a telescopic front fork and twin gas-charged rear shocks. You get the option of a 240mm disc or a 170mm drum at the front with the 130mm drum brake at the rear wheel remaining constant. CBS comes as standard. The front disc is standard in the Split Seat variant.
